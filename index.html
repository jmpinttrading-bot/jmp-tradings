About JMP International Fish Trading

JMP International Fish Trading was established on January 26, 2022, as a sole proprietorship dedicated to the importation and distribution of high-quality fish and seafood products, including frozen seafood and processed products. In just under four years, the company has built a reputation for reliability, compliance, and excellence in the seafood industry, supplying a wide range of clients with products that meet national and international standards.

Driven by a commitment to food safety, traceability, and efficient logistics, JMP International Fish Trading continues to expand its operations, strengthen supplier partnerships, and provide consistent, high-quality seafood solutions, ensuring clients have access to a broad range of products that meet the evolving needs of the market.

Our Mission

Delivering fresh and processed fish and seafood that clients trust, backed by compliance, quality, and efficiency.

Our Vision

To lead the seafood industry as a trusted gateway between global sources and local markets, delivering fresh, frozen, and processed seafood with uncompromising quality, safety, and sustainability.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are your minimum order quantities (MOQ)?

We accommodate dynamic wholesale procurement requests. Orders are systematically tracked and fulfilled either per box or per plastic bundle format depending on the specific product type, target processing cut, and cold-storage distribution specs.

How does JMP verify trade regulatory compliance?

JMP International operates with high-tier documentation verification. All inbound ocean shipments strictly conform to domestic guidelines (including food handling tracking codes) alongside standardized international hazard protocols.

What freezing methodologies are applied to preserve inventory?

Our global fishing assets implement Individual Quick Freezing (IQF) and block-frozen options immediately post-harvest. This process halts nutritional composition breakdown, preventing cell walls from rupturing during cold logistics phases.
